Past Events

  Dr. Prakash Kotecha   October 10th, 2019
Computer Aided Optimization Workshop

The motive of the event was to acquaint our workshop participants with the theory to optimization, different parts of optimization, and types of solution as well with an in-depth knowledge of objective function and understand topics such as constraint satisfying problems. The participants were expected to understand codes on teaching learning based optimization by the end of the lab session based workshop.

  Dr.Anil Kumar Markana   7th November, 2019
LaTeX Workshop

The motive of conducting a workshop on LaTeX was to impart knowledge regarding the importance of a well formatted research paper along with practical coding techniques in-order for our participants to understand and learn coding using latex software for their research work. As LaTeX is a helpful rendering of high-quality typesetting software and is an existing approach for creating documents nowadays. Currently, many academic papers, scientific or technical papers use LaTeX to embrace a top-notch layout. It also eases the readability of a document by enhancing its appearance. After the completion of our workshop participants were to be able to apply LaTeX into their research and academics.

  Dr. Kaustub Sadhekar   9th and 10th November, 2019
Computer Vision Workshop

The motive of conducting the workshop was to impart the necessary and sufficient knowledge over Computer Vision which includes methods for acquiring, processing, analyzing and understanding digital images, and extraction of high-dimension data from the real world in order to produce numerical or symbolic information. The complex and Integrated programme system was delivered in the most simplified manner and with ease to the participants through the workshop.

  Dr.Mazad Zaveri   13th February, 2020
Logisim Workshop

The main objective for this workshop was to bring to the attention of students the importance of learning LogiSim. Main advantage of learning LogiSim includes studying the behavior of a system without building it in reality using different components. Also the results are accurate in general which helps us in forming a general idea about the circuit. The interface itself is very intuitive and the use of color-coding of wires and elements allows for easy analysis and testing of circuits. the completed file can be saved as an image, or as a .circ file. Included circuit components include inputs and outputs, gates, multiplexers, arithmetic circuits, flip-flops, and RAM memory.
